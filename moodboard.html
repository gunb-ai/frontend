A single page to align themes, tokens, type, and UI prototypes for a minimal, technical landing—bonsai brutalism: restraint over spectacle, care over scale; void, moss, clay, sand.

Themes to communicate

Correctness by construction — Invariants live in types and graph shape, not late scans.

Determinism over magic — Pipeline semantics you can reason about; dry-run and tests as first-class.

Structural I/O — The DAG shows where the world is touched; no annotated glue.

Claims follow mechanisms — Prefer what the compiler checks and emits over slogans; humility is naming the boundary honestly.

Humility — Bonsai brutalism: a small, honest surface; pruning and patience over hype. Say what the system does, what it refuses to do, and where the limits are.

Quiet power — Like a gunbai: signal and orchestration, not volume. Strength is in restraint.

Intellectual sustainability — Earthy permanence vs. disposable glue; a ledger, not a funnel.

Assembly-line software — a → b → c as engineering, with explicit artifacts at boundaries.

Visual motifs

Two axes, one story. Left → right = time everywhere it makes sense (pipelines, steps, timelines)—keep that consistent across the page so readers don’t re-learn direction. Downward growth (moss): trunk or main line first, then branches hang under—like the ix ASCII: horizontal spine, then │ / └── forks below. On the web, that lines up with scroll: hero and primary story first; anything optional (tests, dry-run, tangents) lives below as side quests, not competing with the spine for “first read.”

Voice: humble, quiet, precise

  • Humility first. Describe behavior, not destiny. Prefer “is designed to,” “refuses to,” “attempts,” over “guarantees,” “revolutionary,” or unqualified absolutes—unless the math really is there.
  • Quiet confidence. System-contract tone: facts, boundaries, invariants. Let the structure speak; don’t narrate victory.
  • Errors as boundaries, not shame. State the violation calmly (clay)—“compilation halted,” not panic or blame. The compiler is a collaborator with limits, not a judge.
  • Credit tradeoffs. Where something costs complexity or excludes a use case, say so. Bonsai is honest pruning, not pretending the pot is infinite.
  • Visual anti-patterns (same as voice). No purple gradients, big radii, “empower your team,” generic AI hero art—those read as loud sales, not humble tools.
