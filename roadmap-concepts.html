Picking a roadmap visualization

Three candidates. Pick one.

Same data in each — the four-tier roadmap plus omni-ingestion. Different visual shapes. Once you call it, the winner replaces the strip on the home page; the other two get deleted with their styles.

Concept A · capability DAG

The roadmap as the product.

Capabilities are nodes, dependencies are edges. The compiler core is the root; everything else hangs off it. Strongest brand fit ("the product is a graph") and shows real dependencies — e.g. apply_diff builds on fold_node; omni-ingestion is the inverse arrow of translate. Weakness: denser to scan than a list.

Concept B · gantt-style maturity bars

Capabilities along a maturity axis.

One row per capability. Bar starts solid where it lives today and fades into the future. Fastest to read ("where is this thing?") and frames the project as actively moving. Weakness: implies time-to-ship that we don't actually commit to.

Concept C · kanban tier columns

Four columns, capabilities as chips.

Linear / Notion vibe. Each tier is a column; capabilities are chips inside. Easy to scan, easy to add items to, no false time axis. Weakness: looks like a project tracker, not a thesis; doesn't show that capabilities relate to each other.
